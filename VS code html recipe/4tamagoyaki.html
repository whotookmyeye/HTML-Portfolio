Japanese Tamagoyaki

Tamagoyaki is like a warm, cheerful friend—soft, a little sweet, a little savory, and always ready to brighten up your breakfast or bento box with a comforting hug of flavor.

Ingredients

  • 4 Egg
  • 2 tsp Sugar
  • 1 tbsp Mirin
  • 1 tbsp Soy Sauce
  • 1 tbsp Dashi
  • Pinch of Salt
  • Cooking oil

Instructions

  1. Crack the eggs into a bowl, add sugar, mirin, soy sauce, dashi (or water), and a pinch of salt, then gently beat the mixture until well combined but not foamy.
  2. Heat a lightly oiled tamagoyaki pan over medium-low heat.
  3. Pour a thin layer of the egg mixture into the pan, and when it's about 70–80% cooked, roll it to one side using chopsticks or a spatula.
  4. Re-oil the pan, add another thin layer of egg, let it set slightly, then roll the cooked egg back over the new layer; repeat this process until all the egg mixture is used.
  5. Once finished, let the rolled omelette rest briefly, shape it if needed, then slice into even pieces and serve.
