Pork Kakuni: A Hug in Braised Form

Say hello to Pork Kakuni—Japan’s answer to the ultimate comfort food. Think juicy pork belly, slow-cooked in a sweet-savory soy sauce bath until it’s so tender, it practically melts on your chopsticks. It’s cozy, rich, and packed with flavor, like a warm blanket on a rainy day. Whether served with rice, a jammy egg, or a dollop of mustard, Pork Kakuni isn’t just a dish—it’s pure, delicious therapy.

Ingredients

  • Pork belly
  • Soy sauce
  • Sake
  • Mirin
  • Sugar
  • Ginger
  • Green onions (optional)
  • Boiled eggs (optional but highly recommended)

Instruction

  1. Cut the pork belly into big, chunky cubes.
  2. Brown each side o the pork cubes in a hot pan
  3. Add the pork to a pot with just enough water to cover. Bring it to a boil, then lower the heat and simmer for 2-3 hours.
  4. Now in a clean pot, add your pork back along with:
    • 1/2 cup of soy sauce
    • 1/2 cup sake
    • 1/4 cup mirin
    • 2-3 tbsp sugar
    • A few slices of ginger
  5. Add a little water if needed to almost cover the pork. Simmer on low heat for 1.5-2 hours until the pork is buttery soft and full of flavor.
  6. Add soft boiled eggs into the pot during the last 15-20 minutes to let them soak up that liquid gold.
  7. Plate with rice, drizzle some sauce over the top, maybe add mustard on the side for a spicy twist, and enjoy your well earned bowl of cozy
