Pork Sisig

Sisig is one of those iconic Filipino dishes that you just have to try at least once. It originally comes from Pampanga, a province often called the culinary capital of the Philippines—and for good reason. Sisig is a flavorful mix of chopped pork (usually from the pig’s face and ears), seasoned with calamansi, onions, and chili, giving it that perfect balance of savory, tangy, and spicy. It’s often served sizzling hot, sometimes with an egg cracked on top, and it’s a favorite pulutan—or drinking snack—when sharing a few beers with friends. Over the years, it’s gone from a local specialty to a must-have in Filipino restaurants around the world.

Ingredients

  • Pork
    • pork belly
    • pork ears
    • pork face (snout, face, and jowl)
    • liver or liver spread (optional)
  • Onion
  • Garlic
  • Chili pepper
    • siling labuyo (wild chili)
    • siling haba (green long chili)
  • Calamansi
  • Soy Sauce
  • Salt and Pepper
  • Mayonnaise (optional)
  • Fish sauce

Instructions

  1. Boil pork for 30 minutes or until tender.
  2. Grill or fry pork for crispy exterior.
  3. Chop the pork into small pieces.
  4. Chop onion, garlic, and chilis.
  5. Finish with calamansi extract, and top with mayonnaise (optional) before serving.
  6. Pair with rice or alcohol(beer)
